🦿 ● In progress
Chairless Chair — Lower-Limb Passive Exoskeleton

Designing and building a passive lower-limb exoskeleton that allows users to assume a stable semi-seated posture without a chair. A locking linkage mechanism at the knee transfers body weight through the exoskeleton frame directly to the floor, eliminating static muscular load on legs and lower back. Target applications include industrial assembly lines and physical rehabilitation. Designed with an adjustable aluminum frame, 3D-printed articulation joints, and a quick-release buckle system.
